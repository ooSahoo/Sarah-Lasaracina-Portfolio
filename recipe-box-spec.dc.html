Component specification

Recipe Box

The recipe-page layout from Clueless Cooks: a self-contained card that turns a recipe into a checklist. Tools-first, quantities embedded in steps, and a two-colour language separating what goes in from what to do.

  2. BTitle & meta — handwritten display title (Caveat) + read-time eyebrow.
  3. CQuick info — servings, prep, cook & tools. The Tools tile is accented so prerequisites never hide.
  4. DIngredients — shopping list, amounts in orange.
  5. EStep row — number badge, green title, body with embedded quantities, tap-to-check.
  6. FTasting note — handwritten payoff line. The brand’s voice signature.

2 · Colour language

Orange = ingredients & amounts

Any quantity or ingredient inside a step is orange with a soft underline. Lets the eye jump straight to “what & how much” without leaving the step.

Green = technique & warnings

Step titles and any “don’t do this” caution are green — the verbs and the guardrails. Also the checked-state colour.

7 · Behaviour & rules

  • The whole step row is the tap target — one messy-thumb tap toggles checked. Transition ~0.15–0.18s.
  • Checked: surface → green tint, number badge → green, body text → 50% opacity, checkbox fills with ✓. The tap is reversible.
  • Quantities live inside the step (orange), so the cook never scrolls back to an ingredients list mid-task. The top list is for shopping, not cooking.
  • Tools are surfaced in Quick Info, never buried in footnotes — no prerequisite ambushes you mid-recipe.
  • Category pills are persistent and clickable for lateral navigation to similar recipes.
  • Accessibility to-do: the 20px checkbox is below the 44px minimum hit target — the row compensates, but the checkbox alone shouldn’t be the only target.
